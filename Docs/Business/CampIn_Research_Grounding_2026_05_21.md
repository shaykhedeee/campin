# CampIn Research Grounding

Date: 2026-05-21

## What The Market Is Saying

CampIn's wedge remains strong: India has growing adventure/outdoor demand, but the camping experience is still fragmented across operator-led campsites, social recommendations, trekking groups, informal local permissions, and scattered marketplaces.

IMARC estimates India's adventure tourism market at USD 19,708.3 million in 2025, projected to reach USD 88,460.8 million by 2034 at a 17.43% CAGR. This supports a large market direction, but CampIn should still validate a narrow first wedge: Bangalore/South India own-tent, verified campsite, and road-stop demand.

## Reddit And Community Signals

Observed recurring questions:

- Is it legal to camp in forests, beaches, river banks, or public land?
- Can I pitch my own tent near Bangalore?
- Where can I camp safely without local interference?
- Are there places with washrooms, water, parking, and permission?
- Why is Indian camping less structured than countries with formal public campgrounds?

Interpretation:

CampIn should not sell "wild camping freedom" as the promise. The stronger promise is "permissioned, verified, practical outdoor access."

## Competitor Signals

CampMonk, HireACamp, Exoticamp, and other players prove user interest in camping, glamping, villas, treks, cottages, and offbeat outdoor stays.

CampIn's differentiated lane:

- Own-tent camping support.
- Permission-first private land.
- Road-stop network for vehicles and campervans.
- Safety, washroom, water, parking, and emergency-contact transparency.
- Community-led demand validation before booking-engine scale.

## Airbnb Learnings To Adapt

Useful Airbnb principles:

- Trust badges and ranking signals simplify decision-making.
- Host protection and host confidence are part of supply growth.
- App/navigation should keep discovery simple even as categories grow.
- Reviews, reliability, and quality labels are marketplace infrastructure, not decoration.
- Experiences and services show a path beyond stays, but CampIn should not expand before verified camping supply works.

CampIn translation:

- Guest Favorites becomes CampIn Reviewed / Quiet Camp / Founding Host / Road Stop Standard.
- AirCover becomes a future CampIn host protection and incident support framework, but only after legal/insurance review.
- Airbnb Services/Experiences becomes future local add-ons: gear rental, guided hike, outdoor cooking, coffee estate walk, mechanic/road help, but not before pilot traction.

## Official And Compliance Signals

The Ministry of Tourism has guidelines for tented accommodation, which reinforces that camping/tented stays need standards. Goa's caravan policy supports the idea that road-stop/caravan infrastructure can become a formal tourism product. DPDP Act materials reinforce consent, notice, and personal data discipline for waitlists, newsletters, WhatsApp opt-ins, and host data.
