# CampIn User Flows And Testing

## Testing Goal

Before coding more website pages, CampIn should test whether the planned experience makes people feel:

- Understood.
- Safe enough to share contact details.
- Clear on what happens next.
- Willing to pay for permissioned camping.
- Willing to host strangers with rules and support.

## Flow 1: Camper Waitlist

Source:

Reddit, Google, Instagram, friend referral, newsletter forward.

User intent:

I want to camp, but I do not know where it is safe or allowed.

Flow:

1. User sees CampIn promise: safe, permissioned camping around Bangalore/South India.
2. User opens camper waitlist form.
3. User selects city, camping type, own gear status, and top concern.
4. User optionally opts into WhatsApp.
5. User receives welcome email.
6. High-intent leads receive manual founder follow-up.
7. User gets safety survey or route-specific newsletter.

Success criteria:

- 60%+ complete form after starting.
- 30%+ choose WhatsApp opt-in.
- 20%+ answer "yes" to paying for a safe permissioned spot.
- At least 10 high-intent Bangalore leads in first 30 days.

Failure signals:

- Users say the offer is unclear.
- Users think CampIn is already guaranteeing bookings.
- Users do not understand what "permissioned camping" means.
- Users are unwilling to share email.

## Flow 2: Host Application

Source:

Founder DM, Google Maps/business search, Instagram, referral, local network.

User intent:

I may have land or a stay that can host campers, but I need control and safety.

Flow:

1. Host receives founder-led message.
2. Host opens host form.
3. Host sees minimum requirements before entering details.
4. Host submits exact pin, amenities, photos, permission status, and expected price.
5. Founder reviews within 48 hours.
6. Founder schedules call.
7. Qualified host enters verification queue.
8. Strong hosts become Founding Host candidates.

Success criteria:

- 20%+ reply rate from targeted outreach.
- 10%+ of contacted hosts submit a form.
- 50%+ of submitted hosts have washroom and water.
- 10 founding host candidates by day 90.

Failure signals:

- Hosts do not understand camper type.
- Hosts fear damage or police/local issues.
- Host photos are too poor to assess.
- Host wants CampIn to operate the whole campsite.

## Flow 3: Road Stop Lead

Source:

Road-trip forums, Google Maps, overlanding content, local referrals, dhabas/farms.

User intent:

I know or operate a place where road travelers could safely stop overnight.

Flow:

1. CampIn identifies route gap.
2. Lead is added manually or through road-stop form.
3. Amenities are scored.
4. Founder contacts public business number where appropriate.
5. Stop is classified as Candidate, Basic, Standard, or Premium.
6. CampIn does not publish exact trust claims until permission and amenities are checked.

Success criteria:

- 20 candidate road stops in 90 days.
- 5 on Bangalore-Coorg-Wayanad route.
- 3 with confirmed overnight permission.
- 1 pilot overnight stop completed.

Failure signals:

- Stops allow parking but not overnight stays.
- Washrooms are not usable.
- Owners are not reachable at night.
- Route access is unsafe for regular vehicles.

## Flow 4: Newsletter Signup

Source:

Any form, social bio, founder post, partner mention.

User intent:

I want useful camping routes, safety notes, and early CampIn updates without booking yet.

Flow:

1. User submits email.
2. User receives welcome email with CampIn promise.
3. User receives weekly "The Campfire by CampIn".
4. User clicks one of: route guide, safety survey, host a site, reply with a question.
5. Replies become research insights.

Success criteria:

- 35%+ open rate.
- 5%+ click or reply rate.
- 2+ useful replies per issue after first 100 subscribers.

## Usability Test Plan

Participants:

- 5 campers from Bangalore/South India.
- 3 own-gear or road-trip campers.
- 2 first-time or family/women-safety concerned campers.
- 5 host prospects: farm, estate, homestay, road-side business, nature stay.

Format:

Remote 20-minute call or in-person conversation.

Tools:

Prototype form links, screen share, Google Meet/WhatsApp call, note template.

## Camper Test Script

Opening:

I am testing whether CampIn's early signup flow makes camping in India feel clearer and safer. This is not a sales call; I want blunt feedback.

Tasks:

1. Read the waitlist headline and explain what you think CampIn does.
2. Fill the form until the final submit step, but do not submit unless comfortable.
3. Tell me which field made you hesitate.
4. Tell me what information you would need before actually camping.
5. Rank these concerns: safety, permission, washroom, price, crowd, gear, road access.

Questions:

- Would you share your email?
- Would you share WhatsApp?
- Would you pay INR 300-800 for a permissioned own-tent spot with washroom and local host support?
- What would make CampIn feel fake or untrustworthy?
- What route or place would you want first?

Record:

- Completion time.
- Trust objections.
- Confusing words.
- Willingness to pay.
- Preferred next step.

## Host Test Script

Opening:

I am testing whether CampIn's host program is clear and safe for landowners. CampIn is validating demand before building a full marketplace.

Tasks:

1. Read the host pitch and explain what you think CampIn is asking you to do.
2. Review the form fields.
3. Tell me which fields feel difficult or risky.
4. Tell me your top concern about campers.
5. Estimate what you would charge for one tent or one vehicle overnight.

Questions:

- Would you allow own tents?
- Would you require the host/caretaker to be present?
- What rules are non-negotiable?
- What proof do you need from CampIn?
- Would a Founding Host badge or Instagram feature matter?

Record:

- Host control concerns.
- Safety conditions.
- Required amenities.
- Price expectation.
- Permission clarity.

## Acceptance Criteria Before Website Build

CampIn is ready to turn this into website UX only when:

- At least 8 of 10 testers can explain CampIn in one sentence.
- At least 7 of 10 understand the validation-stage promise.
- At least 5 campers are willing to submit email.
- At least 3 campers are willing to share WhatsApp for pilot access.
- At least 3 hosts say they would take a call.
- The top 5 trust objections have answers in copy or process.
