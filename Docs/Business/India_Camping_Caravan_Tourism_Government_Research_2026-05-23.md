# India Camping, Campervan and Caravan Tourism: Government Research Dossier

Research date: 2026-05-23  
Prepared for: CampIn  
Scope: Official Indian central/state tourism sources, current tourism guides, camping/glamping/campervan/caravan material, policy benefits, public-private schemes, and implications for CampIn.

## Executive Summary

India's camping and campervan opportunity is real, but it is fragmented. There is no single national "camp anywhere" permission model. The official landscape is built from:

1. Central Ministry of Tourism guidance for caravan tourism and caravan camping parks.
2. State tourism policies that define caravan parks, camping operators, subsidies, registration, safety and waste rules.
3. State tourism products such as eco camps, tent cities, nature camps, glamping retreats and government tourism corporation stays.
4. Public-private models where government promotes/approves the experience while private operators own or operate the vehicles, camps or parks.

The strongest official signals for CampIn are in Maharashtra, Kerala, Goa, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana, Mizoram, Rajasthan, Uttarakhand, Odisha, Madhya Pradesh and Himachal Pradesh. These states either have caravan/camping policy language, official camping products, or active tourism infrastructure that CampIn can map into a permission-first marketplace.

The biggest gap is also the opportunity: government material focuses on policy and infrastructure, but travelers still need a simple trust layer answering: "Can I legally/peacefully stay here tonight, what facilities exist, who is responsible, and what happens in an emergency?"

## Central Government Framework

### Ministry of Tourism: Caravan and Caravan Camping Parks

The central anchor is the Ministry of Tourism's "Promotion of Caravan Tourism and Caravan Camping Parks" guidance, available through the Ministry website and PDF.

Official source:
- Ministry of Tourism page: https://tourism.gov.in/promotion-caravan-tourism-and-caravan-camping-parks-0
- Ministry PDF: https://tourism.gov.in/sites/default/files/2020-01/Guideline_8.pdf

Core concepts:
- Caravan tourism is positioned as a way to serve remote, eco, adventure, wildlife and pilgrimage destinations where permanent accommodation may be limited or inappropriate.
- A caravan is treated as both transport and accommodation.
- A caravan park is the permissioned stopping/stay infrastructure: parking bays, utilities, toilets, wastewater systems, waste collection, safety/security and tourist facilitation.
- The central guidance becomes a reference document for several states, including Goa, Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Telangana and Mizoram.

What CampIn should extract:
- Minimum host fields: power hookup, freshwater, wastewater disposal, toilets, garbage segregation, lighting, security, reception/contact person, road access, emergency support.
- Classification idea: "overnight caravan park", "day park", "hybrid park", "camping site", "nature camp", "road-stop bay".
- Strong positioning: CampIn should not promote random camping; it should promote verified, permissioned stays and stops.

### NHAI Wayside Amenities

Official NHAI documents show a parallel highway-infrastructure opportunity. NHAI has been developing Wayside Amenities with facilities such as toilets, food, parking, medical rooms and EV charging.

Sources:

CampIn relevance:

## Maharashtra Deep Dive

### 1. MTDC x Motohom Caravan Experience

Official MTDC page:
- https://mtdc.co/en/explore-maharashtra/unique-experiences/caravan-tourism/

Verified from MTDC:
- Maharashtra Tourism Department collaborated with Motohom (Moving Hotels on Wheels) to introduce a caravan experience.
- MTDC presents the experience as a way to explore Western Ghats, Konkan coastal plains and scenic routes.
- MTDC lists caravan parking charges:
  - Single Axle Caravan: Rs. 1,500 + GST
  - Double Axle Caravan: Rs. 2,100 + GST
  - Camper Trailer: Rs. 2,250 + GST
- Safety/comfort features highlighted include live tracking, monitored front/rear cameras, SOS button and siren.

Important wording:
- This should be described as an MTDC/Maharashtra Tourism collaboration with Motohom, not as a claim that the government directly owns every campervan.
- The scheme is best understood as a public-private caravan tourism offering promoted through MTDC.

### 2. Maharashtra Caravan Tourism Policy

Official policy PDF:
- https://www.maharashtratourism.gov.in/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Caravan-Tourism-Policy-english.pdf

Policy aims:
- Implement caravan tourism under the state's tourism framework.
- Provide accommodation at distinctive tourist spots.
- Encourage private investment.
- Support family picnics and nature-proximate stays.
- Offer alternatives to hotels and resorts.
- Develop remote destinations and add value to local tourism economies.
- Use certain "No Development Zones" for tourism facilitation where appropriate.
- Create employment opportunities.
- Provide residence where accommodation is not already available.

Caravan park structure:
- Policy separates "Caravan" and "Caravan Park".
- Caravan parks can be developed mainly on private land by the landowner or developer.
- Minimum land guidance: 1 hectare for up to 20 parking bays.
- Preferred locations include buffer zones of forests, foot of forts, hill stations, dam premises, cave premises, coastal/beach premises and areas near highways.
- Hybrid caravan parks may combine caravan areas with resorts, amusement/adventure/water parks, camping tents, nature parks or agri-tourism.

Government facilitation:
- Help make land available at tourism-potential destinations.
- Help obtain NOCs/permission letters.
- Help register caravan vehicles.
- Coordinate with MTDC, Forest Department, Water Conservation Department, Revenue Department and others.
- Possibility of a single-window process for NOCs/permissions.
- State-level committee for guidance, supervision and regulation.

CampIn opportunity in Maharashtra:
- Build a host pipeline around forts, dams, farms, Konkan coastal areas, Western Ghats, hill stations and highways.
- Offer "CampIn Road Stop" and "CampIn Caravan-Ready Host" checklists aligned to policy: bay size, access, water, power, waste, toilet, emergency contact, local permissions.
- Maharashtra is one of CampIn's strongest states for a pilot because it has both a policy and a consumer-facing caravan product.

## Strong State Models Worth Studying

### Kerala: Responsible Caravan Tourism

Official source:
- https://www.keralatourism.org/keravan-kerala/about

Key points:
- "Keravan Kerala" is the clearest official state-level caravan brand.
- It explicitly links caravans, caravan parks, local communities and Responsible Tourism.
- Tourist caravans require features like sofa-cum-bed, kitchenette, air-conditioning, charging, GPS/monitoring and waste treatment.
- Caravan parks emphasize fencing, security, CCTV, first aid, emergency coordination, waste segregation and sewerage management.
- Kerala allows multiple park formats: state-of-the-art parks, day parks, hybrid resort parks, independent-house parks, plantation parks, KTDC parks and local body/department parks.

CampIn lesson:
- Kerala's model is ideal for CampIn host taxonomy: professional park, day park, private home bay, plantation bay, resort hybrid, government/KTDC bay.

### Goa: Registration and Incentives

Official source:
- https://goatourism.gov.in/caravan-tourism-policy/

Key points:
- Goa defines caravan/campervan broadly: RVs, campervans, motor homes, trailer caravans.
- Caravan parks must be outside restricted zones and provide power, wastewater discharge, freshwater inlet, convenience facility and recreational space.
- Caravan operations must be registered with RTO and Department of Tourism.
- Goa sets NOC expectations for standalone parks: panchayat/municipal trade license, electricity, water, coastal zone clearance if applicable, food/drug, excise if applicable, health, fire and pollution.
- Incentives include capital investment reimbursement, road tax reimbursement and annual registration fee reimbursement for the first 50 caravans under policy conditions.

CampIn lesson:
- Goa is the clearest example of what a compliance-aware onboarding flow should ask for: RTO registration, DoT registration, land/local body approval, fire/health/pollution where applicable, coastal clearance where applicable.

Official sources:
- Tourism Operator Registration System: https://www.tntourismtors.com/

Key points:

CampIn lesson:

## Policy and Incentive Patterns

### 1. Government wants private investment, not only public ownership

Most policies are designed to attract private operators:
- Kerala: private-sector caravan procurement and caravan parks.
- Goa: incentives for first registered caravans.
- Maharashtra: private landowner/developer parks; facilitation from government departments.
- Karnataka: caravan park project subsidies/definitions.
- Tamil Nadu: registration and standardization of operators.
- Telangana: PPP EOI for caravan parks.
- Gujarat: capital subsidy for caravan/campervan/campingvan purchase.

### 2. "Camping" is becoming formal, not free-form

Official language consistently points toward:
- Registered operators
- Permissioned sites
- Waste management
- Fire/health/pollution approvals where applicable
- Power, water and sewage infrastructure
- Tourist safety and security

CampIn should not build around "hidden spots"; it should build around verified hosts.

### 3. Hybrid caravan parks are a practical Indian model

Hybrid parks are attractive because they reduce risk:
- Existing resorts, farms, dhabas, homestays, eco parks and agri-tourism properties can add dedicated caravan/camping bays.
- Travelers get toilets, staff, power, food, security and emergency contact.
- Hosts earn incremental revenue without building a full resort.

CampIn's strongest host product should probably be "verified overnight bay + toilet + staff contact", not only full campsites.

### 4. Wastewater is the make-or-break infrastructure

Central and state policies repeatedly mention wastewater discharge, sewerage connection, sewage collection/treatment and waste segregation. This is the operational weakness that separates real caravan parks from marketing.

CampIn host field to add:
- Freshwater available: yes/no
- Greywater disposal: yes/no/how
- Blackwater/sewage disposal: yes/no/how
- Waste bins: biodegradable/non-biodegradable/recyclable
- Toilet type: flush/bio-toilet/dry/none

### 5. Local permissions vary sharply

Especially sensitive zones:
- Forest buffers and wildlife areas
- Coastal/beach areas
- Hill stations and landslide-prone zones
- Dam premises
- Cave/archaeological premises
- Border and permit states
- Riverbanks and flood-prone areas

CampIn should use a "permission confidence" label rather than making universal legal claims.

## Risks and Claims to Avoid

Avoid:
- "Camping is legal anywhere in India."
- "You can park and sleep anywhere on public land."
- "Forest/beach/riverbank camping is allowed" unless a specific authority/operator permits it.
- "Government rents campervans directly" for Maharashtra; say MTDC/Maharashtra Tourism collaborated with Motohom unless a government-owned rental vehicle is specifically verified.
- "Caravan-ready" unless water, waste, toilet, power and overnight permission are confirmed.

Use:
- "Permission-first camping."
- "Verified host sites."
- "State rules and local permissions vary."
- "Check current permissions before travel."
- "Official policies show growing support for caravan parks and formal campsites."
