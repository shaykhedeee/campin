# The Campfire By CampIn

## Purpose

"The Campfire by CampIn" is the weekly trust and validation engine. It should make people feel CampIn is useful before the marketplace exists.

Audience:

- Campers waiting for safe spots.
- Hosts curious about listing land.
- Road travelers and own-gear campers.
- Partners watching the outdoor category.

## Newsletter Promise

One useful email every week about safe camping, road stops, private-land stays, and the people building India's outdoor culture.

## Issue 001

Subject:

The Campfire #001: Why safe camping near Bangalore is harder than it should be

Preview:

CampIn is mapping permissioned camping, starting with Bangalore, Coorg, Wayanad, and Chikmagalur.

Body:

Hi,

CampIn is starting with a simple belief: camping in India should not feel like guesswork.

The problem is not that people do not want to camp. They do. The problem is that too many people are unsure where they are allowed to pitch a tent, whether the place has a usable washroom, whether locals will object, and whether the site is safe after dark.

So we are starting in Bangalore and South India, mapping permissioned camping spots and road stops one by one.

This week we are looking for:

- Own-tent campers near Bangalore.
- Farms, estates, and homestays around Coorg, Wayanad, Chikmagalur, Ramanagara, and Kanakapura.
- Road stops with washrooms, water, parking, and a local contact.

Safety note of the week:

Do not treat random public land as a campsite. The safest early CampIn standard is simple: clear permission, exact pin, washroom access, water, and a reachable host.

Community question:

If you could get one safe permissioned camping spot within 3 hours of Bangalore, what would matter most: privacy, washroom, bonfire, price, views, or host presence?

Reply to this email with your answer.

Stay. Explore. CampIn.

## Issue 002

Subject:

The Campfire #002: The own-tent question

Preview:

CampIn is testing whether people want to bring their own tents, not just book pre-made camps.

Body:

Hi,

One of the strongest signals from early research is that many campers do not want a packaged trip. They want to bring their own tent, cook simply, sit quietly, and not be bothered.

That is exactly the gap CampIn wants to solve.

The hard part is not the tent. It is permission.

For own-tent camping to work in India, a place needs:

- Clear host permission.
- Usable flat ground.
- Washroom access.
- Water.
- Basic night safety.
- Arrival instructions.
- Local rules.

This week, we are asking hosts a simple question:

Would you let verified campers pitch their own tent on your land if CampIn helped with discovery, rules, and communication?

If you know a farm, estate, homestay, or road-side business that might say yes, reply with the name or location.

Stay. Explore. CampIn.

## Newsletter Rules

Do:

- Be practical.
- Share what CampIn is learning.
- Ask one useful question.
- Make the reader feel part of the build.
- Keep links focused.

Do not:

- Overpromise verified places.
- Hide the validation phase.
- Send generic destination listicles.
- Use inflated "revolution" language.
- Spam people more than once per week unless they opted into a specific flow.
