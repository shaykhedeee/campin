# CampIn Host Agreement Draft

Draft version: 2026-05-21

This draft sets expectations for founding hosts, private landowners, farm stays, estates, resorts, cafes/dhabas, and road-stop partners.

## 1. Host Role

The host provides accurate information about the property or stop and agrees to host CampIn users only when permission, access, amenities, and safety expectations are clear.

## 2. Required Host Information

Hosts must provide:

- Property type.
- Exact location pin.
- Permission or ownership/operation status.
- Washroom availability.
- Water availability.
- Parking and vehicle access.
- Electricity/power availability, if any.
- Photos.
- Expected price.
- Local contact person.
- Emergency notes.
- Restrictions: pets, fires, alcohol, music, arrival time, group size, weather limits.

## 3. Verification

CampIn may classify a host/site as:

- Unverified.
- Host-declared.
- Founder-call reviewed.
- Photo reviewed.
- CampIn Reviewed.
- Pilot-ready.

Hosts may not use CampIn badges unless approved by CampIn.

## 4. Host Standards

Hosts should:

- Keep promised amenities available.
- Make prices and extra charges clear.
- Avoid overbooking.
- Respect camper privacy and safety.
- Provide access instructions before arrival.
- Keep a basic issue-resolution process.
- Inform CampIn immediately if permissions, access, water, washrooms, or safety conditions change.

## 5. Payments

For manual pilots, payment handling must be agreed before confirmation. CampIn should log:

- Total price.
- Host payout.
- CampIn fee, if any.
- Refund terms.
- Payment status.

## 6. Cancellations

Hosts should avoid last-minute cancellations except for safety, weather, permission, emergency, or operational reasons. Repeated unreliable hosting may lead to delisting or loss of badge status.

## 7. Incidents

Hosts must report:

- Injury.
- Harassment.
- Theft.
- Property damage.
- Local conflict.
- Wildlife risk.
- Fire risk.
- Police/authority interaction.
- Amenity failure.

## 8. Termination

CampIn may pause or remove a host for misleading information, unsafe conditions, repeated complaints, hidden charges, harassment, or legal/permission concerns.
