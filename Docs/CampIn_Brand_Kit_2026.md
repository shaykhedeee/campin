# CampIn Brand Kit 2026

## Brand Essence

CampIn makes camping in India feel safe, human, and possible.

It is not luxury travel. It is not roughing it without trust. It is the middle path: real outdoor stays, verified hosts, road-trip utility, and community confidence.

## Primary Logo Idea

The new mark combines:
- A tent for camping.
- A map-pin silhouette for verified places.
- A small route line for road stops.
- A sunrise/fire accent for warmth.

Usage:
- Use the full lockup in the website nav, decks, invoices, and proposals.
- Use the icon-only mark for favicon, app icon, WhatsApp profile, Instagram profile, and map pins.
- Keep enough clear space around the mark so it feels calm and premium.

## Voice

CampIn should sound:
- Warm, not cute.
- Trustworthy, not corporate.
- Practical, not over-poetic.
- Indian, but not tokenistic.
- Premium through clarity, not luxury language.

Use:
- verified
- road stop
- host
- camper
- farm
- forest
- terrace
- private land
- washroom
- water
- safe parking
- WhatsApp
- UPI
- local support

Avoid:
- sanctuary as the default word
- displaced soul
- luxury unless describing a specific tier
- resort
- disrupt
- revolutionize
- "best kept secret" cliches

## UI Direction

The website should feel like a useful marketplace first and a brand world second.

Preferred UI:
- Search and filters are prominent.
- Listing cards show price, location, verified amenities, and road-stop status.
- Host economics are clear.
- Safety and verification are visible before the user is asked to trust.
- Buttons are direct and readable.

Avoid:
- Huge all-caps poetic sections.
- Too many nested cards.
- Decorative blobs, orbs, and heavy gradients.
- Stock-photo feeling without real utility.
- UI labels that feel like fantasy language.

## Photography

Use images that show:
- Actual camping setups.
- Indian farms, hills, rivers, deserts, orchards, terraces, or road corridors.
- People and hosts where possible.
- Clear amenities when trust matters.

Avoid:
- Dark, vague atmosphere.
- Generic alpine imagery that does not feel Indian.
- Ultra-luxury glamping as the default.
