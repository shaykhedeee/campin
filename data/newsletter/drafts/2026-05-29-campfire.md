Hi,

Late May is when weekend routes start colliding with monsoon reality: wet ground, flooded approach roads, and “it’ll be fine” plans that become stressful after sunset.

This week’s CampIn takeaway is simple: the first layer of trust is not more listings. It’s fewer surprises—starting with permission authority, then the basics that make an overnight stop workable.

## 1) What CampIn Learned This Week

In our current lead set, *every* promising host-style lead still has a critical gap: it’s unclear who has the authority to approve overnight parking/tents on the exact patch of land. Water and washroom are the most common claims, but they need current proof (photos or direct confirmation) before we treat them as reliable.

## 2) Practical Camping Answer: The “Permission-First” 5 Questions

If you’re calling a property, homestay, farm, or “camp” before you go, ask these in one WhatsApp/call:

1. “Who can officially approve an overnight stay here?” (owner/manager/leaseholder)
2. “Can you share the exact Google Maps pin for *the parking/camping spot*?”
3. “Do guests have washroom access all night?” (not only staffed hours)
4. “Is *drinking water* available, or only utility water?”
5. “If we arrive late, who answers and where do we check in?”

If any answer is vague, treat it as a day-visit plan—not an overnight plan.

## 3) Route / Location Note (a useful direction, not a recommendation)

Two supply pockets look unusually strong for “structured” camping infrastructure right now:

- **Kerala’s official caravan-park inventory** (examples in our research: Palakkad/Malampuzha and Vagamon/Idukki) looks like rare, purpose-built supply for campervans + water/power/waste handling.
- **The Bengaluru → Mandya/Malavalli corridor** has multiple BYOT-style properties in the pipeline where host-direct contact exists, but campervan acceptance, parking layout, and permission authority still need confirmation.

If you’re planning a monsoon trip, prefer routes where you can keep a *clean exit option* (paved turnaround + known halt) and don’t bet your night on an unverified “forest edge” pin.

## 4) Trust / Host Insight: What “CampIn-reviewed” means (and what it doesn’t)

During validation, “CampIn-reviewed” means we manually try to confirm: permission status (who controls the stay area), exact pin, washroom/water reality, reachability, and any arrival-before-dark / quiet-hour rules. It does **not** mean we are guaranteeing safety, legality, or on-ground conditions—those change by season and operator.
