## Direct Answer

A real campervan road stop in India is a **permissioned overnight bay** with predictable basics: safe access/turning space, a confirmed check-in contact, toilets open at night, drinking/utility water, lighting/security, clear waste rules, and an exit plan for bad weather. If any one of these is unclear, treat it as “not a stop yet.” CampIn labels stops by verification stage, not hype.

## Why This Matters Now (May 2026)

Two trends are colliding:

- **Caravan travel is being formalised** through policies and park guidance (the “minimum infrastructure” picture is getting clearer).
- **Road-trip + vanlife demand is rising faster than reliable overnight infrastructure**, so travellers fall back to guesswork (“we’ll find a spot”), which increases risk, conflict, and bad outcomes for hosts and communities.

## The CampIn Road-Stop Standard (What We Record Before Calling It a “Stop”)

Think of this as the same discipline you’d want in a hotel booking—just adapted for vehicles.

### 1) Permission & proof (non-negotiable)

- Who explicitly allows overnight halts here (name + role)?
- What is allowed: sleeping in vehicle vs pitching a tent vs “no outside setup”?
- Proof you can show: booking, receipt, permit, written message, or official listing.

CampIn note: this is not legal advice; rules and enforcement vary. We treat “unclear” as “no” until verified.

### 2) Vehicle fit (most “failures” happen here)

- Vehicle type supported (SUV, campervan, caravan trailer).
- Approach road: width, slope, sharp turns, low branches/wires, gate width.
- Turning/exit plan: can you leave at night if needed?
- Surface + drainage: mud risk in monsoon; ground firmness for jacks/levelling.

### 3) Night operations (what matters at 1:00 AM)

- A reachable check-in contact and backup number.
- Lighting coverage over the bay.
- Staff/guard presence (or clearly stated “no staff after X”).
- Emergency path: nearest hospital/24×7 help + who helps you get there.

### 4) Toilets, water, and hygiene

- Toilets usable at night (not “locked after dinner”).
- Water: drinking vs utility water (separate if possible).
- Basic hygiene: handwash, waste bin, and a realistic cleaning routine.

### 5) Power, charging, and noise expectations

- Power availability (if offered): outlet type, hours, pricing, and load limits.
- Generator policy: allowed/quiet hours.
- Quiet hours and neighbour sensitivity (avoid conflict with nearby residences).

### 6) Waste and “leave no trace” rules (protect access)

Minimum expectations (especially if marketed as a “caravan park” or “overnight stop”):

- Clear trash disposal (bins + where it goes).
- Greywater/blackwater policy (what is allowed, what is not).
- Fire/cooking rules and local restrictions.

## A Simple Stop-Grading System (So We Don’t Overpromise)

CampIn can label every stop as one of these (visible on the listing/page):

1) **Lead**: public place identified, no confirmation.
2) **Contacted**: host/operator replied, permission claim exists, proof pending.
3) **Permission-verified**: written permission + rules captured.
4) **Facility-verified**: toilets/water/night operations confirmed (photo/video or third-party proof).
5) **Recently checked**: re-confirmed within a defined freshness window (e.g., last 60–90 days).

This avoids the common trap of “working number” claims that turn stale quickly.

## What To Ask Before You Drive There (Copy/Paste Message)

Send this to a host/operator (WhatsApp/SMS):

- “Do you allow **overnight halts** for a campervan on (date) from (time) to (time)?”
- “Can we use the **toilet at night**? Is it open 24×7?”
- “Is there **lighting** and someone on-site at night?”
- “Any rules: generator, cooking, pets, quiet hours?”
- “Please share the exact **parking bay location** and gate/approach instructions.”

If they can’t answer these clearly, treat it as “not ready.”

## Where “Real Stops” Are Most Likely to Emerge First (India)

These are *categories*, not endorsements:

- **Hybrid caravan parks** on existing hospitality properties (hotels/estates/resorts), where toilets, staff, and security already exist.
- **Designated caravan parks** built with infrastructure expectations (water, electricity, waste handling).
- **Formal highway amenities / wayside amenities** where longer halts are explicitly allowed (always confirm on-site—design intent ≠ overnight permission everywhere).
- **Permissioned private properties** with caretaker + toilet access (small farms/estates) that can offer one or two bays responsibly.

## Seasonal Note: Monsoon Turns “Okay on Paper” Into “Stuck in Mud”

For May–September travel (especially Western Ghats and Kerala routes), make drainage and exit-plan questions mandatory:

- Surface type, slope, and waterlogging history.
- Whether the approach road floods/collapses in heavy rain.
- Recovery options (tractor/tow availability) and what it costs.

## FAQs

**Q: Can I sleep in a campervan anywhere in India?**

A: Don’t assume that. The safest approach is **explicit permission** (private property / managed park / designated facility). If permission or rules are unclear, choose a different stop.

**Q: What’s the minimum viable road stop for a campervan?**

A: Permission proof, safe access/exit, **toilet at night**, water, lighting/security, and a reachable on-site contact.

**Q: Are wayside amenities meant for overnight halts?**

A: Many are designed for “rest and refreshment” with parking and toilets, but overnight rules can vary by location. Always follow signage and confirm with staff.

**Q: Should CampIn enable instant booking for road stops now?**

A: Not until verification workflows and host operations are stable. Early-stage trust beats speed.
