## A Permission-First Checklist for Guests (Book Like You Mean It)

Before you pay, ask for proof you can screenshot and keep:

### 1) Ownership + on-site responsibility

- Who is the owner/manager (name + phone), and who is on-site overnight?
- Is this registered/recognized under Karnataka’s tourism registration system (if applicable)?

Tip: Karnataka Tourism publishes an “official list” of registered homestays—use it as a credibility check when a property is marketing itself as a homestay-style stay.

### 2) Boundaries (the “don’t wander into trouble” test)

- A Google Maps pin for the gate + the camping zone.
- A simple boundary note: “Where can we walk after dark? Where should we not go?”

### 3) Facilities that separate “romantic” from “risky”

Minimum for most guests:

- toilets (count + type), bathing option, and drinking water arrangement
- lighting on paths to toilets
- on-site waste collection (trash bags + where it goes)
- phone network reality (which carriers work, if any)

### 4) Fire/noise/alcohol rules (write it down)

- Are bonfires allowed? Only in a fixed pit? Until what time?
- Quiet hours: what time does noise stop?
- Alcohol policy: allowed/limited/prohibited?

If rules are “we’ll see,” don’t go.

## Host Guide: How to Set Up Coffee Estate Camping Without “Grey-Zone Tourism”

This is not legal advice—use it as a practical operations checklist. If you’re unsure, get written clarification from the competent authority before listing.

### Step 1: Decide your model (and your compliance path)

Pick one primary product and build around it:

- **Homestay with a designated camping corner** (guests primarily book rooms; optional outdoor experience)
- **Tented accommodation as the main stay** (guests primarily book tents)
- **BYOT (bring your own tent) pitch-only** (you provide a spot + facilities + caretaker)

Why this matters:

- Karnataka’s tourism ecosystem regulates “tourism trade” and sets expectations for recognized operators in regulated destinations.
- If you are offering tents as accommodation (not just a picnic), align your setup with India’s Ministry of Tourism tented accommodation guidelines for baseline quality, safety, and documentation.

### Step 2: Get your “paper trail” in order (trust is a product)

Have these ready before you accept bookings:

- land ownership/lease documents for the hosting entity
- a written site map (guest areas, staff areas, restricted areas, water source points)
- emergency plan: nearest clinic, police station, forest office (if relevant), and a contact sheet
- guest ID capture + basic entry/exit register

### Step 3: Build “quiet safety” infrastructure (not just aesthetics)

What experienced guests silently judge:

- clean toilets with lighting and a defined cleaning schedule
- safe night movement: path lights, reflective markers, basic signage
- cooking/bonfire zone separated from tents (and a clear no-fire fallback if conditions are dry/windy)
- wildlife-aware waste handling (sealed bins; no food waste near tents)

### Step 4: Put your rules in writing (and enforce them)

Publish your rules in three places:

- on the listing
- on a WhatsApp pre-arrival message
- on a printed sign at the site

Non-negotiables that prevent incidents:

- quiet hours
- no off-boundary wandering after dark
- bonfire policy (fixed pit only, supervision, cutoff time)
- “leave no trace” checklist for guests

## FAQs

### Is coffee estate camping in Coorg legal?

It’s most defensible when it’s **explicitly hosted on private land** with the landholder’s permission, clear boundaries, and safe facilities. If a property is forest-adjacent or implies access “inside forest,” assume activities may be regulated and get explicit, written permission from the competent authority rather than relying on informal norms.

### Do I need to register if I’m hosting guests on my estate in Karnataka?

Karnataka has a tourism trade regulation framework, and the state tourism department publishes guidance and official lists for registered operators (e.g., homestays). If you’re operating a guest accommodation business (rooms, tents-as-stays, or similar), plan for registration/recognition/classification as applicable and avoid operating as an unverified “informal campsite.”

### If I provide tents, what standards should I follow?

Use the Ministry of Tourism’s **tented accommodation classification** guidance as a baseline for documentation and quality expectations (even if you also meet local requirements). It’s a practical checklist for what guests—and regulators—expect from a tent stay marketed as accommodation.

### Are bonfires okay on estates?

Only if the host explicitly permits it and provides a controlled setup (fixed pit, supervision, cutoff time, water/sand nearby). If conditions are windy/dry or the host can’t manage it safely, skip the bonfire and provide lantern lighting + a common seating area instead.
